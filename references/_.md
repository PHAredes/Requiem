## üìö Textbooks

### Foundation

**[Types and Programming Languages (TAPL)](https://www.cis.upenn.edu/~bcpierce/tapl/)** - Benjamin C. Pierce (2002)
- MIT Press
- The standard introduction to type systems
- Key chapters: 5-8 (Untyped lambda calculus), 9-11 (Simply typed lambda calculus), 22-23 (Type reconstruction, universal types)

**[Advanced Topics in Types and Programming Languages (ATTAPL)](https://www.cis.upenn.edu/~bcpierce/tapl/)** - Pierce (ed.) (2004)
- MIT Press
- Chapter 2: **Dependent Types** (Aspinall & Hofmann) - directly relevant to Requiem
- Chapter 1: Substructural Type Systems

**[Proofs and Types](http://www.paultaylor.eu/stable/prot.pdf)** - Jean-Yves Girard, Yves Lafont, Paul Taylor (1989)
- Cambridge University Press
- Covers proof theory, Curry-Howard correspondence, System F
- Explains propositions-as-types foundation underlying Requiem's identity types

### Online Textbooks

**[Programming Language Foundations in Agda (PLFA)](https://plfa.github.io/)**
- Philip Wadler, Wen Kokke, Jeremy Siek (2024)
- Complete development from lambda calculus through type soundness proofs in Agda
- Part 1 (Logical Foundations): Properties like confluence and normalization that Requiem tests
- Part 2 (Programming Language Foundations): Type systems and metatheory

**[Certified Programming with Dependent Types (CPDT)](http://adam.chlipala.net/cpdt/)** - Adam Chlipala (2013)
- Practical proof engineering in Coq
- Chapter 12: Higher-Order Abstract Syntax (explains Requiem's HOAS approach)

## üõë Termination and Structural Recursion

**["A Predicative Analysis of Structural Recursion"](https://www.cse.chalmers.se/~abela/jfp02.pdf)** - Andreas Abel and Thorsten Altenkirch (2002)

- Introduces a more general principle for termination based on tracking size changes.
- Key reference for advanced termination analysis.
